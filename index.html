The Obstructive Charm of the Unfamiliar

Most of the images of African people living in tribes are framed by the far removed Western-perspective, which depicts them almost exclusively for the Western world’s consumption. Despite the hypnotic and fascinating charm of the images, these representations let us perceive different African tribes as a homogenous group with general characterizations and features. Our eyes linger on the unfamiliar objects, the colorful clothes, the unusual jewelries: all symbols of what stand outside the conventional or established understandings we normally experience. Our perception on them is the involuntarily answer we give to what is unknown, since the “Other” has always represented a source of fear and fascination; an emblem of difference demonized and romanticized. The ‘exoticism’ becomes the distracting and dividing element on which most of our focus of attention lays. The portraits, no matter their quality and intrinsic beauty, tend to reduce the characters of the pictures as mere displays of exoticism, ‘objects’ of fascination to observe from a distance. The publication is an attempt to investigate and visually illustrate such phenomenon.

Unstructured Structures

By exploring different aspect of our civilizations, cultures and beliefs this hybrid publication attempts to uncover the invisible structures our bodies live in. Ethical and cultural structures have always had a major role in the way we perceive ourselves and the way we want to be seen.
'Unstructured Structures' explores the reasons why people feel the need to create such strict guidelines in order to feel accepted.

Project done in collaboration with Zahari Dimitrov.

Realising Sexes–Intersecting Conversations on Intersex

Intersex isn’t selective, it occurs consistently and everywhere. But even though there is a sense of universality in the fact that sex varies, there is definitely not one unified discourse on the topic. In fact, the subject of intersex is still largely unknown territory to most people. The information that is public and available tends to belong to one of two genres: medical papers, in which any definitive conclusion seem impossible while keeping it objective, and personal accounts delivered as activism within NGO articles. ‘Realising Sexes’ tries to bridge the gap and position itself between the subjective and factual. It is an effort to open up a space for intersecting discourses on this shared subject of intersex. By inviting voices from different fields and juxtaposing their contributions, a dialogue arises that hasn’t necessarily happened in physical space on a topic that is both social and medical, sensitive and urgent.

Project in collaboration with Nicolai Schmelling.

Almanac of Communication Tools

Alongside the printed page, written language is represented in various forms. Public space is filled with messages of all kinds written by the individuals that inhabit it. Through these interventions of self-expression, different forms of written and oral language have been born that go unaccounted for in the history of communication. Each of these methods has its own formal and conceptual qualities, as well as its own history and background. These tools represent an untold history of graphic design that has existed and developed alongside the popular communication tools of print publishing, radio, imagestelevision and the internet.

In my freetime...

...I like to photograph lonely gloves I find on my way. Someone once made me think of how many of them are laying around all alone and abandoned during winter season. While framing the solitary hand that almost looks in a cry for help, my mind wonders on the other glove; the ‘lucky’ one. I like to imagine it somewhere warmer, still safe in someone’s pocket thinking about its lost companion…
Friends heard about this passion of mine and started sending me pictures of their findings.
